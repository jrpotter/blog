    Common.Real.Geometry.Rectangle #

    A characterization of a rectangle. This follows the definition as outlined in [^1]. Note that a Point and a LineSegment are both considered rectangles, with one or both dimensions equal to 0 respectively.

    [^1]: Apostol, Tom M. Calculus, Vol. 1: One-Variable Calculus, with an Introduction to Linear Algebra. 2nd ed. Vol. 1. 2 vols. Wiley, 1991.

    A Rectangle is characterized by three distinct points and the angle formed between line segments originating from the "bottom left" point.
